I am a second-year PhD student in Political Science at the University of Montreal (Canada) under the supervision of Ruth Dassonneville. My dissertation explores the role of local factors on citizens’ assessments of the nation’s economic performance.

Substantively, my research focuses on electoral accountability and, more specifically, economic voting in established democracies. I am also interested in questions on representation, the political consequences of housing markets and the effect of electoral outcomes on policymaking. Methodologically, I have a keen interest for causal inference, experimental methods in social sciences and smart identification strategies.

I am currently a research fellow at the Canada Research Chair in Electoral Democracy led by Ruth Dassonneville and affiliated with the Center for the Study of Democratic Citizenship.

I am also on Twitter @alexjabbour.
